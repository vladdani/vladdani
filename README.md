<h2 align="center">I learn fast, build fast, and care about making useful products feel great.</h2>

<p align="center">
  Product manager by craft, builder by habit. I like listening closely, finding the real problem, and turning new tools into products people can actually use.
</p>

---

All this time I was a Product Manager, but the world is changing so fast that the title matters less and less. Some people call this kind of work product, some call it engineering, and some call it forward deployed engineering.

I don't really care what the role is called. I like building things, listening to customers, understanding what is actually painful, and making products that solve the problem well. When I learn a new tool, pattern, or technology, I want to use it immediately on something real and keep pushing until it feels clear, useful, and polished.

Lately that has meant building across AI-native workflows, media tools, document processing, local-first Mac utilities, and product analytics.

### Recent builds

| Product | What it is |
| --- | --- |
| **Raytrace** | A Raycast extension for tracking AI usage across Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and API providers from one place. |
| **Tamaclawchi** | A Mac companion experiment that gives favorite AI tools a personality people can relate to, beyond terminal mascots and developer-only interfaces. |
| **Tidy Takes** | A local media workflow app with batch processing, dedupe, templates, and NLE export improvements. |
| **Getplace tools** | Dashboards and internal products for reviews, promos, brand analytics, CRM workflows, and market intelligence. |
| **Document processing systems** | AI-assisted extraction and structured processing for legal, finance, and operational documents. |

### What keeps showing up in my work

- **Product taste** - interfaces should be fast, obvious, and pleasant to use.
- **Useful AI** - AI should remove busywork, open a workflow, or make something meaningfully easier.
- **Fast learning loops** - learn the tool, build with it, ship the rough version, then tighten the product.
- **Customer closeness** - the best ideas usually come from watching where people struggle.
- **Craft in the small details** - onboarding, names, empty states, speed, and interaction quality matter.

Most of my recent work is in active private repos, but the activity is real: I am building, testing, renaming, polishing, and shipping constantly. The public page is a window into the direction, not the full source dump.
