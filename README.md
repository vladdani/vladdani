## I learn fast, build fast, and care about making useful things feel great.

All this time I was a Product Manager, but the world is changing so fast that the title matters less and less. Some people call this kind of work product, some call it engineering, some call it forward deployed engineering. I don't really care what the role is called. I like building things, listening closely to customers, understanding what is actually painful, and making products that solve the problem well.

Lately I've been building across AI-native workflows, media tools, document processing, local-first Mac utilities, and product analytics. The common thread is simple: learn whatever is needed, apply it immediately, and keep pushing until the tool is genuinely useful.

### Recent builds

- **Raytrace** - a Raycast extension for tracking AI usage across Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, and API providers.
- **Tamaclawchi** - a Mac companion experiment that gives favorite AI tools a personality people can relate to, beyond terminal mascots and developer-only interfaces.
- **Tidy Takes** - a local media workflow app with batch processing, dedupe, templates, and NLE export improvements.
- **Getplace tools** - dashboards and internal products for reviews, promos, brand analytics, CRM workflows, and market intelligence.
- **Document processing systems** - AI-assisted extraction and structured processing for legal, finance, and operational documents.

### What I care about

- Product taste: interfaces that are clear, fast, and pleasant to use.
- High-impact shipping: building the thing, testing it with real workflows, and iterating quickly.
- Practical AI: not demos for their own sake, but AI where it removes busywork or unlocks a new workflow.
- Native-feeling tools: small details, onboarding, speed, and interaction quality.
- Continuous learning: picking up new stacks and patterns by applying them directly to real products.

Most of my recent work is in active private repos, so the public page is more of a window into what I build than a full source dump.
